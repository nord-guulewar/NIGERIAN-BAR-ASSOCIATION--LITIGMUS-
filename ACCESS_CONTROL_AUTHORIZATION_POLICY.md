# Access Control and Authorization Policy - NBA LITIGMUS

**Effective Date:** June 25, 2026

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how access to NBA LITIGMUS is granted, managed, reviewed, restricted, and revoked.

---

## 2. Principles

Access must follow:
- Least privilege
- Need-to-know access
- Role-based and, where applicable, attribute-based restrictions
- Separation of duties for sensitive functions
- Timely revocation when access is no longer justified

---

## 3. Role-Based Access Structure

NBA LITIGMUS supports multiple operational roles, including administrators, registrars, judges, clerks, finance personnel, bailiffs, secretaries, records staff, litigation officers, prosecutors, probate officers, librarians, and other authorized court personnel.

Access should be limited according to the role matrix, assigned jurisdiction, court, division, and operational responsibility.

---

## 4. Access Lifecycle

### Provisioning
- Access must be approved through an authorized process
- Accounts must be tied to a defined role and institutional purpose

### Review
- Access rights should be reviewed periodically
- Privileged accounts require heightened review

### Modification
- Access must be updated when a user's role, court, department, or responsibility changes

### Revocation
- Access must be revoked or suspended promptly upon termination, reassignment, misconduct, or security risk

---

## 5. Privileged Access

Administrative or high-risk access must be limited, monitored, and logged. Elevated access should only be used where operationally necessary.

---

## 6. Authentication and Session Security

Accounts must be protected through appropriate authentication controls, secure credentials, and monitored access behavior. Shared accounts are not permitted unless expressly approved and controlled.

---

## 7. Enforcement

Unauthorized access attempts, privilege misuse, or access control violations may result in suspension, investigation, reporting, disciplinary action, or referral under applicable institutional or legal procedures.

---

## 8. Related Controls

This policy should be read together with the Information Security Policy, Terms of Use, Privacy Policy, and relevant technical access controls implemented in the application and database layers.
