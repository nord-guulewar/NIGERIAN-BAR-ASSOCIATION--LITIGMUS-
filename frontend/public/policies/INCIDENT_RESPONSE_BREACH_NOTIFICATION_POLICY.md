# Incident Response and Data Breach Notification Policy - NBA LITIGMUS

**Effective Date:** June 25, 2026

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how NBA LITIGMUS handles security incidents, suspected breaches, confirmed breaches, and related notification obligations.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to incidents involving:
- Unauthorized access
- Credential compromise
- Data loss or exposure
- Malicious activity or misuse
- Service disruption affecting confidentiality, integrity, or availability
- Third-party incidents affecting platform data

---

## 3. Response Stages

### Identification
- Detect and document the incident
- Preserve relevant evidence and logs
- Classify the severity and affected systems

### Containment
- Isolate affected systems, accounts, or integrations
- Limit further exposure or unauthorized activity

### Investigation
- Determine scope, root cause, affected data, affected users, and timeline
- Assess legal, contractual, and regulatory impact

### Remediation
- Patch vulnerabilities
- Reset credentials where necessary
- Restore systems safely
- Strengthen controls to prevent recurrence

### Recovery
- Resume operations in a controlled manner
- Validate integrity and security before full restoration

### Post-Incident Review
- Document findings
- Update procedures and controls
- Track corrective actions

---

## 4. Notification

Where required by applicable law, affected institutions, regulators, or individuals must be notified within applicable legal timelines. The platform's Nigerian compliance posture should consider prompt breach assessment and, where mandated, notification obligations such as the 72-hour expectation referenced in Nigerian and international privacy frameworks.

Notifications should include, where appropriate:
- Nature of the incident
- Categories of data affected
- Likely impact
- Containment and remediation steps
- Recommended protective actions for affected persons

---

## 5. Escalation

Material incidents must be escalated to appropriate security, legal, privacy, administrative, and executive stakeholders.

---

## 6. Recordkeeping

All incidents and response actions should be documented for compliance, learning, and audit purposes.

---

## 7. Testing and Review

This policy should be exercised periodically through tabletop reviews, incident drills, or operational simulations.
