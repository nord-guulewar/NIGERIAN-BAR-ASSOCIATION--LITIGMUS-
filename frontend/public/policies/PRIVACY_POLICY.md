# Privacy Policy - NBA LITIGMUS

**Effective Date:** June 25, 2026

---

## 1. Introduction

NBA LITIGMUS ("we," "our," or "us") is a case management system developed for the **Nigerian Bar Association (NBA)** and related court operations. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, store, and protect personal information.

This policy is designed to support compliance with applicable privacy and data protection requirements, including:
- **Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA)**, the **Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR)**, and related Nigerian data protection obligations
- **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** where applicable to individuals in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or other jurisdictions with equivalent rights
- **California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)**, where applicable to California residents

This document is provided for operational transparency and platform governance. It should be reviewed with legal counsel before public production publication.

---

## 2. Scope of This Policy

This Privacy Policy applies to personal information processed through NBA LITIGMUS in connection with:
- User account registration and authentication
- Court administration and case management workflows
- Scheduling, document handling, and notifications
- Payment processing and financial recordkeeping
- Security monitoring, audit trails, and regulatory compliance

This Privacy Policy applies to users and data subjects regardless of nationality, descent, place of birth, citizenship, or naturalization status where their personal information is processed through NBA LITIGMUS. This includes Nigerian users and international persons whose personal information is processed in connection with Nigerian court, legal, administrative, or institutional workflows supported by the platform.

The fact that a person is not Nigerian by descent, birth, citizenship, or naturalization does not by itself exclude them from protections described in this policy. The applicable legal framework will depend on factors such as the location of the person, the jurisdiction of the relevant court or institution, the location of processing activities, and the laws that govern the relevant records or services.

This policy does not replace court-specific filing rules, judicial confidentiality rules, legal privilege obligations, or statutory record-retention duties.

## 2A. Nigerian Data Protection Framework

Because NBA LITIGMUS is operated for Nigerian legal and court-related use cases, Nigerian data protection law is a core part of the platform's governance model.

For purposes of this policy:
- The **Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA)** is the principal modern statutory framework for personal data protection in Nigeria.
- The **Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) 2019** remains an important compliance reference and historical regulatory foundation for privacy governance, accountability, lawful processing, and data subject rights in Nigeria.
- Guidance, directives, or enforcement expectations issued by the **Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC)** may also inform how privacy obligations are interpreted and implemented.

This means that where NBA LITIGMUS processes personal information as part of Nigerian court administration, legal services, judicial records, or related operational functions, Nigerian data protection requirements may apply even when the relevant person is not Nigerian, provided the processing has a sufficient connection to Nigerian operations, institutions, systems, or legal obligations.

At the same time, if a non-Nigerian person is located in another jurisdiction with stronger or additional privacy protections, we may also need to comply with those laws to the extent they apply to the processing in question.

## 5. How We Use Personal Information

We use personal information to:
- Create and manage user accounts
- Authenticate users and secure access to the platform
- Register, manage, assign, and track cases
- Process payments and maintain court-related financial records
- Schedule hearings and send notifications
- Generate operational, compliance, and management reports
- Maintain audit trails and incident records

We do not use personal information for unrelated commercial profiling or consumer advertising.

Where GDPR or similar laws apply, we rely on one or more of the following legal bases:
- **Legal obligation:** Compliance with court administration, judicial recordkeeping, anti-fraud, audit, and regulatory duties
- **Performance of a contract:** Providing the platform and related services to authorized organizations and users
- **Legitimate interests:** Security, platform integrity, fraud prevention, incident response, service improvement, and administrative oversight
- **Consent:** Where consent is specifically requested and is the appropriate legal basis
- **Vital interests or public interest:** Where applicable to judicial, legal, or emergency matters

Under the NDPA, NDPR, and related Nigerian requirements, processing may also be based on legal obligation, contractual necessity, consent, legitimate interests, public interest, or other lawful bases recognized under applicable Nigerian data protection rules, subject to appropriate safeguards.

## 8. How We Share Personal Information

We do **not** sell personal information.

We may disclose personal information to:
- **Authorized court and NBA personnel:** Judges, registrars, clerks, secretaries, records officers, accountants, cashiers, bailiffs, and other approved personnel based on role and need-to-know access
- **Legal participants:** Lawyers or other authorized case participants where case workflow requires access
- **Service providers:** Hosting, email, communications, backup, monitoring, and infrastructure vendors acting on our behalf
- **Regulators, courts, law enforcement, or other authorities:** Where required by law, court order, or lawful process
- **Professional advisers:** Auditors, compliance reviewers, legal counsel, or security consultants under confidentiality obligations
- **Successors or institutional reorganizations:** If operational control of the system changes, subject to lawful handling requirements

We do not share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.

## 11. Data Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this policy, including legal, regulatory, judicial, security, accounting, and operational obligations.

Typical retention principles include:
- **Active accounts:** Retained while the account remains active and operationally necessary

- **Audit and security logs:** Retained for security operations, investigations, and compliance review
