# Data Retention and Deletion Policy - NBA LITIGMUS

**Effective Date:** June 25, 2026

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how NBA LITIGMUS retains, archives, deletes, anonymizes, and preserves data processed through the platform.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- User account records
- Case and judicial workflow records
- Payment and financial records
- Audit logs and security logs
- Notifications and operational metadata
- Uploaded documents and related evidence records
- Backups, archives, and exports

---

## 3. Retention Principles

Data must be retained only for as long as necessary for legal, judicial, regulatory, accounting, security, and operational purposes.

Retention decisions should consider:
- Court or statutory recordkeeping obligations
- Litigation hold or investigation requirements
- Security monitoring and incident response needs
- Audit and financial accountability obligations
- Operational continuity and disaster recovery needs

---

## 4. Standard Retention Categories

### User Accounts
- Active user accounts: retained while access remains operationally necessary
- Deactivated accounts: retained for role history, audit, and security review for a defined administrative period
- Deleted accounts: removed or anonymized when no longer required by law, audit, or security obligations

### Case and Judicial Records
- Case files, judgments, hearing schedules, service records, and court workflow data: retained according to applicable court rules, institutional schedules, and legal obligations
- Records subject to appeal, dispute, or review: retained until all relevant processes are complete and any required preservation period expires

### Payment and Financial Records
- Payment records, receipts, transaction references, and related financial controls: retained for accounting, reconciliation, fraud review, audit, and regulatory compliance

### Audit and Security Logs
- Audit logs: retained for at least 3 years unless a longer retention period is required
- Security logs and incident records: retained as needed for investigations, breach analysis, trend review, and compliance evidence

### Backups and Archives
- Backups: retained according to operational recovery needs and secure rotation schedules
- Archived data: retained only where required for legal, institutional, or continuity purposes

---

## 5. Deletion and Anonymization

Where data is no longer required, it should be:
- Securely deleted
- Irreversibly anonymized
- Archived under controlled access if deletion is not yet permitted

Deletion must be suspended where data is subject to:
- Legal hold
- Investigation
- Court order
- Security review
- Regulatory preservation requirement

---

## 6. User Requests

Requests for deletion or erasure must be reviewed against legal, judicial, security, and recordkeeping obligations. A request may be denied, limited, or deferred where retention remains necessary.

---

## 7. Responsibilities

Relevant administrators, compliance personnel, legal stakeholders, and system operators must ensure retention schedules are applied consistently and reviewed periodically.

---

## 8. Review

This policy should be reviewed at least annually and whenever legal or operational requirements materially change.
