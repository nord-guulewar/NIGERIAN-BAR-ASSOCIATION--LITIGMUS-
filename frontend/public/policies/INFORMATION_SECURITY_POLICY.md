# Information Security Policy - NBA LITIGMUS

**Effective Date:** June 25, 2026

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the baseline information security requirements for NBA LITIGMUS.

---

## 2. Objectives

The platform must protect the confidentiality, integrity, availability, and accountability of:
- Personal information
- Case and judicial records
- Financial and payment information
- Administrative and security records
- Platform configuration, infrastructure, and logs

---

## 3. Security Controls

Security controls for NBA LITIGMUS include:
- Strong authentication and role-based or attribute-based access control
- Secure password handling and credential protection
- Encryption in transit and secure storage practices
- Logging, audit trails, and security monitoring
- Input validation and application-layer protection against common web attacks
- Rate limiting, abuse prevention, and incident detection controls
- Backup, recovery, and continuity measures
- Controlled infrastructure, deployment, and configuration management

---

## 4. Access Security

Access must follow least-privilege principles. Administrative and privileged access must be restricted, reviewed, and revoked promptly when no longer required.

---

## 5. Data Security

Sensitive and regulated data must be protected through technical and organizational safeguards appropriate to the risk, the legal context, and the nature of the records involved.

---

## 6. Monitoring and Logging

Security-relevant events should be logged and reviewed regularly, including authentication events, privileged actions, record access, failed access attempts, and incident indicators.

---

## 7. Vulnerability and Change Management

The platform should be maintained through secure updates, dependency reviews, configuration hardening, and periodic security assessment.

---

## 8. Third-Party Risk

Service providers that store, transmit, or process platform data must be assessed and managed under appropriate contractual, confidentiality, and security obligations.

---

## 9. Incident Readiness

Security controls must support timely detection, containment, investigation, and reporting of incidents.

---

## 10. Governance

This policy should be read together with the Privacy Policy, Incident Response and Data Breach Notification Policy, and Access Control and Authorization Policy.
